<p><p>The Germanium wafer market includes two primary types based on conductivity: P-type and N-type. P-type Germanium wafers are doped with elements that create "holes," allowing for positive charge carriers. They are used in applications like solar cells and diodes. N-type wafers, on the other hand, are doped with elements that contribute free electrons, facilitating negative charge flow. Both types play crucial roles in semiconductor technology, with diverse applications in electronics, photonics, and communication systems, reflecting their specialized functionalities.</p></p>

<p><p>Blockchain platforms can be categorized into three main types: public, private, and consortium. Public blockchains are open and decentralized, allowing anyone to participate and verify transactions, fostering transparency. Private blockchains, on the other hand, are restricted to specific organizations, enhancing control and privacy for sensitive data. Consortium blockchains lie between the two, where a group of organizations shares control, enabling collaborative efforts while maintaining some level of access restrictions, ideal for industries requiring shared governance.</p></p>

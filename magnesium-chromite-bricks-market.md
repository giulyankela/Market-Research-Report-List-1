Sintering involves heating the raw materials to fuse them without melting, resulting in a dense, durable product suitable for demanding environments. Direct-bonded bricks, on the other hand, are produced by bonding magnesia and chromite at high temperatures, offering superior thermal stability and chemical resistance.

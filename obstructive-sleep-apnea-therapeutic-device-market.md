<p><p>The obstructive sleep apnea therapeutic device market includes two primary types: polysomnography (PSG) and pulse oximeters. PSG devices conduct comprehensive sleep studies by monitoring brain waves, oxygen levels, heart rate, and breathing patterns, helping diagnose sleep disorders. Pulse oximeters, on the other hand, are non-invasive devices that measure blood oxygen saturation levels, providing essential data for assessing apnea severity. Together, these devices enable improved diagnosis, monitoring, and management of obstructive sleep apnea, enhancing patient outcomes.</p></p>
